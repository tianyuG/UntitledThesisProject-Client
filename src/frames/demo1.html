Hugh, 15th Baron Willoughby of Parham

Hugh, 15th Baron Willoughby of Parham (1713 â€“ 17 January 1765) was an English nobleman and hereditary peer of the House of Lords. He was born in 1713, the eldest son of Charles Willoughby, 14th Baron Willoughby of Parham and Hester, daughter of Henry Davenport of Little Lever and Darcy Lever, near Bolton. He was born at a poor age and is said to have been a strong father-son, having been educated in the Royal College of Art and at Oxford University. He had no other experience as a child but was educated at the school of law in the North. He was also a naturalist, having attained great importance in literature at the Duke College.
He was a great intellectual, having been taught both in English and in English . He was the first in English to be called a British man of many years. His first novel, 'Wake me up' was published in 1755. He was an enthusiastic reader of literature, and was highly intelligent. He had no other interest in literature except for the first novel which he read. In 1761 he took a commission in the French journal De la Recherche du Maroc and published 'The Great Poet', which in 1764 was made into a play.
